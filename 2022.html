Material Ecology in the Symbiocene

In this space, you encounter a vision for a regenerative future- one where humanity and the natural world perform in symbiosis. Material Ecology in the Symbiocene presents materials and objects engineered by, for, and with nature. The work reveals knotty and strange interactions between environment and organism; human and non-human ecologies, that co-evolve and disseminate knowledge and memory through a place. Derived from the sea and the earth, these materials and the ecologies they inhabit surpass man-made design through their sustainability, circularity, and adaptability.

Mycelium, the vegetative part of a fungus, is essential to the health of ecosystems, aiding in the decomposition and regeneration process. Mycelium can be “grown” into any form and possess self-healing properties. Seaweed is among the fastest-growing organisms on the earth, a storer of CO2 emissions, and a biofilter for chemical pollution.

An olfactory piece, blended by the artist in collaboration with a perfumer, transports us to the forest where the Oyster mushroom grows.

Through life and decay, there is a coming-together of organisms that build on and complexify each other before falling apart. Upon their end of life, the materials will return to the soil to support ecosystem health and new growth.

The work is motivated by continuous exchanges and ever-changing relationships with ecologists and mycologists, seaweed, plants, and fungi.
